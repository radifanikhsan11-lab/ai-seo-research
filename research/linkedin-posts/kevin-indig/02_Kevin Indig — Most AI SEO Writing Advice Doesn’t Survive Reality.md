## Content (cleaned)

Most AI SEO writing advice does NOT survive real-world conditions.

When analyzing writing signals across different industries, performance varies heavily depending on the vertical.

What works in one niche can fail in another.

For example:
- SaaS content behaves differently from Finance content
- Structural signals are not universal across industries

---

## Core Finding

There is no single “AI SEO writing formula” that works everywhere.

However, one signal consistently performs well across all verticals:

→ A **direct declarative opening sentence**

Examples:
- “[X] is [Y]”
- “[X] does [Z]”

---

## What Works (Universal Signals)

- Direct opening statements improve AI citation likelihood
- Clear declarative structure reduces ambiguity
- Fast extraction of meaning improves AI visibility

---

## What Does NOT Work

- Hedging language in introductions
- Overly generic “best practice” structures
- One-size-fits-all content templates
- Ignoring vertical-specific content expectations

---

## Key Insight

AI systems do not evaluate content uniformly.

They evaluate based on:
- industry context
- structural clarity
- extractability of meaning

---

## AI / SEO Implication

- SEO is becoming **vertical-sensitive**, not universal
- Writing structure must adapt per industry
- AI favors clarity over stylistic variation
- “Best practice SEO writing” is no longer universal

---

## Actionable Takeaway

- Adapt content structure per niche (don’t reuse templates blindly)
- Start articles with direct, factual statements
- Remove hedging in introductions
- Optimize for extractability, not just readability
